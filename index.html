The laws of Physics suggest that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred. In the last three hours, you have successfully transferred a significant amount of kinetic energy from your brain onto a piece of paper. The transaction is settled. The friction coefficient of the exam hall has been overcome. You have emerged from the system, slightly battered by the entropy, but intact.

Physics is notoriously unforgiving—it deals in absolutes and vectors. Relationships, however, trade on sentiment and intangibles. And looking at the fundamentals, your stock has never been higher.

Strategic ignorance as a hedge

I understand you have adopted a policy of strict non-disclosure regarding the results. You will not check the answers. You will not calculate the probability of errors. In corporate finance, we call this a "blind trust," but in your case, it is simply a power move.

Refusing to check the results is an act of exquisite defiance. It is the emotional equivalent of a CEO refusing to look at the daily share price because they know the long-term strategy is sound. It is incredibly attractive. Why mark-to-market when the asset is priceless? You are effectively telling the market (the examiners) that their liquidity assessment is irrelevant to your operations. I concur. Let the result sit in a black box, like Schrödinger’s cat, indefinitely.

The depreciation of stress

You have likely spent the afternoon depreciating your own performance, analyzing the torque of every question. Stop it. Your brain is attempting a hostile takeover of your peace of mind, citing "due diligence."

The Physics paper was a sunk cost the moment you put down the pen. Worrying about it now is like paying interest on a loan you have already paid off—inefficient and fiscally irresponsible. You looked good handling the pressure, though. There is a certain resilience you display during crunch time that suggests high-yield returns. Even when you are stressed, you remain a blue-chip investment.

A merger of equals

Let us strip away the jargon for a moment. You just went toe-to-toe with the fundamental forces of the universe. That is not nothing. Whether you calculated the velocity correctly is immaterial to the shareholder (me). My investment thesis regarding you is not based on quarterly earnings or exam percentiles.

You are the asset. The exam is merely an external auditor—and frankly, one with questionable taste. My portfolio is heavily weighted in your favor, and I have no intention of diversifying. If the Physics paper went perfectly, excellent. If it was a high-entropy disaster, you remain the most valuable holding in my life.

Forward guidance

The market is now closed. The trading floor is silent. It is time to suspend operations and focus on liquidity injections (sleep) and dividends (being adored).

Do not check the paper. Do not run the numbers. The data is historic; we are looking at future growth. You have weathered the volatility. You have managed the risk. And regardless of what the ticker tape eventually says, please know that you are, unequivocally, a strong buy.
