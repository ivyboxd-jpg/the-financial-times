The Geography of Missing You

On exams, state lines, and why I’m politely asking you to drop the highlighter.

By Niv
March 11, 2026

I am looking at you right now through a screen, and I’m just going to say it: you are an absolute menace when you’re in the zone. You’ve got that deeply unserious, hyper-focused crease between your eyebrows. You are currently surrounded by an ungodly amount of paper, highlighters that have definitely seen better days, and a cup of something you made two hours ago and promptly abandoned for the sake of English literature.

It’s highly distracting, honestly. You sitting there, wrapped up in the gravity of tomorrow’s exam, completely unaware of how incredibly gorgeous you look when you’re quietly strategizing. I am sitting here, a few states away, fighting the very strong, very irresponsible urge to book a flight immediately just so I can steal all your pens and force you to pay attention to me instead.

But I am exercising superhuman restraint, because I know what tomorrow is.

Tomorrow is the English exam. Three hours of sitting at a wobbly desk, trying to convince some terribly underpaid examiner that you deeply, spiritually resonate with the metaphor of a tree. I know how your mind works. And because I know you, I know exactly what kind of mental gymnastics are happening behind those pretty eyes right now. You’re not just studying. You are meticulously plotting an academic revenge arc.

The last three exams were, for lack of a better word, profoundly rude. You walked out of those halls knowing exactly what you were capable of, only for the paper to stubbornly refuse to cooperate. And because you are you—fierce, stubbornly brilliant, and maybe just a tiny bit competitive—you’ve decided that tomorrow is where you even the score. You’ve decided you need to absolutely annihilate this English paper to "make up" for the others.

Can we just pause for a second and appreciate how incredibly hot it is that you care this much? Seriously. The sheer force of your willpower is terrifying in the best way possible. But my god, Aaradhya, can we also acknowledge how wildly unnecessary it is for you to carry this much stress on your shoulders?

Long distance is hard enough without the CBSE catching strays and stressing out my girl. Before I give you my incredibly biased, entirely unsolicited advice for tomorrow, I actually hacked the NYT games section twice just to prove a point. Play these first.

See? That’s what actually matters. You’re sitting there keeping quiet when I know your brain is running at a hundred miles an hour, cataloging themes and character sketches and poetic devices. You’re treating tomorrow like it’s a trial by fire. Like if you don’t walk out with full marks, the universe is going to send you a strongly worded email of disappointment.

I need you to listen to me, across all these miles and all these stupid state lines: you don’t owe this exam a damn thing.

You don't owe it a perfect score. You don't owe it a comeback story. The syllabus is just an artificial construct, and frankly, I think it’s just intimidated by you. Think about how absurd it is to take someone like you—someone who is naturally witty, who notices the tiniest, most beautiful details about the world, who makes me feel like the luckiest person alive—and ask you to flatten all that vibrant intelligence into bullet points.

I wish you could see yourself the way I see you. If there were an exam on how deeply you care about the people in your life, you would break the grading curve. If they handed out marks for the way your laugh completely changes the energy in a room, or the way you fiercely defend your opinions, they’d run out of paper.

I’m telling you this because I want to interrupt that little voice in your head right now. The one that’s telling you that everything hinges on tomorrow. That voice is a liar. You are fiercely intelligent. You have put in the work. All those other people's last lessons are tucked behind the stress, waiting to be used. You are going to sit down tomorrow, and you are going to handle it, just like you handle everything else—with absolute grace.

But even if you don't? Even if the paper is weird, or you forget a quote? It changes literally nothing. I will still be right here, entirely obsessed with you. I will still think you are the most fascinating, beautiful, brilliant girl to ever walk the earth. You are going places that no mark sheet could ever map out, and I am just ridiculously happy I get a front-row seat to watch you do it.

So here is my official advice for the rest of your Tuesday: finish the chapter you’re on. Highlight whatever needs highlighting. And then, please, close the book. Step away from the desk. Let your brain breathe.

You have absolutely nothing to prove tomorrow. You are already enough. Take a deep breath, gorgeous. You’ve got this. And even when you feel like you don't, I’ve got you.
